## Domain 1: ADDICTION

**Existing frameworks:** Disease model treats addiction as chronic brain pathology. Learning model treats it as deeply encoded habit. Berridge's incentive salience shows wanting/liking dissociate. Koob & Le Moal's allostatic model tracks hedonic set-point drift.

**R=C-A application:** Addiction is progressive A inflation. The substance becomes an agenda that distorts processing of all incoming contact. Critically, C often remains high or increases (addicts confront harsh realities constantly — withdrawal, consequences, social damage), yet R remains low because A absorbs the contact.

**Novel prediction:** Theorem 2 predicts addiction is a **Class (ii) stable state**. This means partial recognition ("I know I'm an addict") is an *attractor*, not a step toward recovery. The framework formally predicts that *insight alone cannot break addiction* — not because habits are strong (learning model) or because the brain is diseased (disease model) — but because Class (ii) is mathematically stable without architectural change to f.

Specific falsifiable prediction: r(B) — the marginal recognition return on additional contact — should be measurable. During active addiction, r→0. Interventions that increase C alone (confrontations, evidence of harm) should show near-zero r(B). Interventions that modify the processing architecture (behavioral restructuring, environmental change, 12-step's "higher power" as externalized processing) should show r(B) recovery. This predicts that **experience-based interventions should outperform insight-based interventions**, which is borne out clinically but is *predicted* here rather than observed post-hoc.

**Existing coverage:** Koob's allostatic model explains hedonic shift but not the epistemic trap. Berridge explains wanting≠liking but not why knowing this doesn't help. The Class (ii) stability prediction — that the stuck state is a formal attractor requiring architectural escape — goes beyond all four frameworks.

**Assessment:** (c) Falsifiable and novel — r(B) as measurable diagnostic, Class (ii) stability as formal explanation for why insight fails.

**Depth: DIAGNOSTIC** — identifies the precise mechanism (architectural stability) that existing frameworks gesture at but don't formalize.

## Domain 2: PROPAGANDA

**Existing frameworks:** Bernays/Lippmann focus on information management and opinion engineering. Chomsky-Herman identifies five structural filters that constrain what reaches the public. Ellul distinguishes integration propaganda (long-term environmental conditioning) from agitation propaganda.

**R=C-A application:** Propaganda can attack either term. Chomsky-Herman's filters reduce C (what reaches the audience). Ellul's integration propaganda increases A (shapes the processing architecture). R=C-A unifies these as two strategies against a single equation.

**Novel prediction 1:** The framework predicts that the **most effective propaganda maximizes C while maximally loading A in a correlated way**. Censorship (reducing C) is detectable — people notice absence. But agenda-loading (high A, high C) creates the illusion of high R. The target believes they are well-informed precisely because C is high, while r(B)→0. This predicts that open societies with heavy narrative framing should exhibit *lower average R* than the population's high C suggests — a measurable information-theoretic prediction.

**Novel prediction 2:** Axiom 2 generates a **formal impossibility result for complete propaganda immunity**. Any media literacy framework is itself a processing architecture with A* > 0. The propaganda detector has its own agenda floor. This predicts that media literacy training should show diminishing returns asymptoting at a positive error rate — not approaching zero. No existing propaganda framework formally proves this impossibility.

**Existing coverage:** Ellul comes closest with his argument that propaganda is inescapable in technological societies, but he argues this sociologically, not information-theoretically. The formal impossibility result is novel.

**Assessment:** (c) The impossibility bound is falsifiable (test whether media literacy training asymptotes) and novel.

**Depth: DIAGNOSTIC, bordering GENERATIVE** — the impossibility result is genuinely novel. The C/A correlation prediction refines Ellul but doesn't fully transcend existing work.

## Domain 3: SCIENTIFIC REVOLUTION

**Existing frameworks:** Kuhn's paradigm shifts describe incommensurability and anomaly-driven revolution. Lakatos's research programmes have a hard core with protective belt. Popper's falsificationism prescribes conjecture and refutation.

**R=C-A application:** Normal science: A is constant (paradigm = fixed f), C increases through experiment, R increases as r(B)≈1. Anomaly phase: C keeps increasing but A absorbs it — r(B) drops toward 0. Crisis: the C-R gap becomes undeniable. Revolution: new f adopted, A drops, R jumps.

**Novel prediction:** R=C-A predicts that scientific revolutions should be triggered when **r(B) hits zero** — the point where no additional experimental contact yields additional recognition within the current architecture. This is more precise than Kuhn's "anomaly accumulation" because it specifies a measurable quantity. Kuhn says anomalies pile up; R=C-A says you can identify the exact moment the paradigm becomes informationally opaque.

Theorem 5 (Recognition Cascade) predicts that within a paradigm, R_n ≤ R_{n-1} for scientists building on each other in a pure chain. This predicts **decreasing marginal originality within paradigmatic lineages** — a testable prediction about scientific productivity more precise than Lakatos's "degenerating programme" (which describes the programme, not the individual-level cascade).

**Existing coverage:** Lakatos's concept of degenerating programmes is close to the r(B)→0 prediction. The cascade theorem adds individual-level resolution but the programme-level pattern is already identified.

**Assessment:** (b)-(c) The r(B) metric is potentially operationalizable but would require significant methodological development. The cascade theorem prediction is falsifiable in principle.

**Depth: DIAGNOSTIC** — adds precision and individual-level resolution to Kuhn/Lakatos but the core dynamics are well-captured by existing frameworks.

## Domain 4: PSYCHOTHERAPY

**Existing frameworks:** CBT modifies distorted cognitions. Psychodynamic makes the unconscious conscious. ACT increases psychological flexibility through defusion. Person-centered provides unconditional positive regard as growth conditions.

**R=C-A application:** Each therapy targets a different component:
- CBT → reduces A_bias (cognitive distortions as specific agenda items)
- Psychodynamic → attempts to observe A itself (make agenda conscious)
- ACT → modifies f (defusion = architectural change to processing)
- Person-centered → increases C (unconditional regard = contact without agenda-loading)

**Novel prediction 1: Formal impossibility of complete therapeutic resolution.** Axiom 2 proves that A* > 0 for any self-modeling system. This means ALL therapies must asymptote at a positive floor — not because of practical limitations (therapist skill, patient motivation, problem complexity) but because **self-observation necessarily has positive noise**. No existing therapeutic framework formally proves this. CBT, psychodynamic, ACT, and person-centered all implicitly treat complete resolution as theoretically possible. R=C-A says it is mathematically impossible. This is a different claim from "therapy has limits" — it's "the limits are provable and non-negotiable."

**Novel prediction 2:** Theorem 2 predicts different long-term trajectories depending on intervention type. Therapies that only modify cognitive content (reducing specific A_bias items) should produce Class (ii) plateaus — stable partial recognition. Therapies that modify processing architecture (ACT's defusion, genuine psychodynamic restructuring) should show different curve shapes — potentially jumping between classes. This predicts that **ACT and structural psychodynamic work should show stepped improvement curves, while pure CBT should show smooth asymptotic curves**. This is testable with longitudinal outcome data.

**Novel prediction 3:** The r(B) metric applied to therapy predicts that therapeutic contact (sessions) should show measurable marginal recognition return. When r(B)→0, the current therapeutic approach has saturated. This gives a formal criterion for when to change therapeutic modality — an actionable clinical prediction.

**Existing coverage:** The "common factors" literature finds all therapies roughly equally effective, but doesn't explain why or predict ceilings. The Dodo bird verdict is descriptive; R=C-A provides a structural explanation (all therapies converge on the same A* floor).

**Assessment:** (c) The impossibility result is falsifiable — test whether therapeutic improvement curves are consistent with a hard asymptote vs. a soft power-law decay. The different-curve-shapes prediction across modalities is directly testable.

**Depth: GENERATIVE** — the impossibility proof and the differential trajectory prediction are genuine novelties that no existing therapeutic framework produces.

## Domain 5: MEDITATION

**Existing frameworks:** Buddhist phenomenology describes progressive dissolution of self-view through stages of insight. MBSR operationalizes attention training for stress reduction. Britton's deconstructive meditation examines meditation as perceptual deconstruction. Predictive processing accounts frame meditation as precision-weighting modification.

**R=C-A application:** Meditation systematically reduces A while maintaining or refining C. Shamatha (concentration) = stabilizing the contact channel, reducing noise. Vipassana (insight) = observing the processing architecture itself, reducing A by making A_bias visible.

**Novel prediction:** Axiom 2 generates a **formal disagreement with classical Buddhist soteriology**. Some traditions (Theravada's arahantship, Dzogchen's rigpa, some Zen claims) assert that complete liberation — A=0 — is achievable. R=C-A formally proves this is impossible: A* > 0 for any self-modeling system. The self-observation floor means that the most advanced meditator retains irreducible agenda.

This maps to the Buddhist concept of "subtle obscurations" but goes further: it's not that subtle obscurations are difficult to remove, it's that they are **impossible to remove entirely** while remaining a self-modeling system. To reach A=0 would require ceasing to self-model, which would mean ceasing to be a recognizer at all.

Falsifiable prediction: Long-term meditators should show diminishing returns on perceptual accuracy measures that asymptote at a positive error floor. If a meditator could be shown to achieve zero self-referential distortion on a well-designed task, this would falsify Axiom 2 as applied.

**Existing coverage:** The predictive processing account captures most of the A-reduction dynamics. The Buddhist phenomenological tradition acknowledges the difficulty but not the impossibility. The formal impossibility is novel.

**Assessment:** (c) Falsifiable (though difficult to test) and novel — directly contradicts liberation claims of several contemplative traditions.

**Depth: DIAGNOSTIC** — the impossibility result is genuinely novel, but most of the descriptive mechanics are well-captured by predictive processing. The single novel prediction (impossibility of A=0) is powerful but narrow.

## Domain 6: FALLING IN LOVE

**Existing frameworks:** Attachment theory frames romantic love as adult attachment behavior. Sternberg decomposes love into intimacy + passion + commitment. Fisher identifies neurochemical phases (lust, attraction, attachment). Evolutionary psychology focuses on mate selection optimization.

**R=C-A application:** Falling in love involves a **simultaneous massive spike in both C and A**. Contact is intense (you attend to the other person with extraordinary focus). But agenda is also massive (idealization, projection, limerence, desire). The crucial question: what happens to R?

**Novel prediction 1: The experience of "truly seeing someone" during infatuation is a recognition illusion.** R = C - A. If C and A both spike proportionally, R may stay flat or even decrease. The subjective feeling of "I really see you for who you are" during infatuation corresponds to high C, not high R. The person is maximally *contacted* but not maximally *recognized*. This directly contradicts the phenomenology of falling in love and offers a falsifiable reframing.

Testable: measure perceptual accuracy about a partner's actual traits (not idealized traits) during infatuation vs. six months later. R=C-A predicts accuracy should be *lower* during infatuation despite the subjective experience of maximal insight.

**Novel prediction 2: Arranged vs. romantic marriage trajectories.** R=C-A predicts specific R-curves. Romantic love marriages: high initial C and A, R starts moderate or low, then either increases (if A drops faster than C) or collapses (if C was maintained only by A). Arranged marriages: lower initial C and A, R starts low, then increases monotonically as C grows and A stays low. The framework predicts **convergence from different trajectories** with arranged marriages showing more monotonic R-growth. There is some empirical support for satisfaction convergence, but the *mechanism* (different C/A dynamics) is novel.

**Novel prediction 3:** Theorem 2 applied to relationships: mature love stabilizes in Class (ii). Complete mutual recognition is impossible (Axiom 2). The experience of "being fully known" is always partially illusory. This predicts a measurable gap between *perceived* mutual understanding and *actual* mutual understanding even in the best relationships — a prediction no existing love framework makes formally.

**Existing coverage:** Attachment theory focuses on security, not accuracy. Fisher's neurochemistry describes the chemicals but not the epistemic consequences. Sternberg's triangle has no informational content. None of them predict that falling in love *reduces* recognition.

**Assessment:** (c) The recognition-illusion prediction is directly falsifiable and genuinely novel. The arranged vs. romantic trajectory prediction is testable and mechanistically novel.

**Depth: GENERATIVE** — the prediction that infatuation involves a recognition *decrease* despite the subjective experience of maximum recognition is a genuinely novel claim that no existing framework makes.

## Domain 7: MARKET BUBBLES

**R=C-A application:** During a bubble, C is extremely high (massive information processing, 24/7 market data) but A is also high (profit motive, confirmation bias, social proof, career risk of dissenting). R remains low despite enormous C because A absorbs the information.

**Novel prediction:** The r(B) metric provides a **formal bubble diagnostic**. During a bubble, r(B)→0: additional market information produces zero additional recognition. This is distinct from all existing approaches:
- Minsky identifies the *dynamic* but not a real-time metric
- Shiller identifies the *psychology* but not a formal bound
- EMH says bubbles are unidentifiable
- Soros describes the *feedback* but not when it saturates

R=C-A predicts that r(B) — the marginal recognition return on new information — is the key diagnostic. A bubble is formally identifiable as r(B)≈0 regardless of price level. This could in principle be operationalized through information-theoretic analysis of how market prices respond to genuinely novel data.

**Existing coverage:** Information cascade theory (Banerjee, Bikhchandani) already captures herding formally. Soros captures reflexive dynamics. The r(B) metric is a refinement, not a revolution.

**Assessment:** (b)-(c) Interesting and potentially operationalizable but would require significant methodological development to test.

**Depth: DIAGNOSTIC** — the r(B) bubble metric is valuable and more precise than existing tools, but the fundamental dynamics are well-captured by Minsky + Soros + behavioral finance.

## Domain 8: RADICALIZATION

**Existing frameworks:** Significance quest theory (Kruglanski) frames radicalization as a search for personal significance. 3N model identifies needs, narratives, and networks. Moghaddam's staircase describes progressive escalation. Social identity theory explains in-group/out-group dynamics.

This is not reducible to "brainwashing" (low C) or "irrationality" (bad processing). It's high C with high correlated A — the same signature as the most effective propaganda.

**Novel prediction 1: Class (ii) stability of radicalization.** Once A exceeds a threshold and the system enters Class (ii), Theorem 2 predicts it is *stable*. Deradicalization requires architectural change to f, not just counter-narratives (which are more C within the same processing architecture). This formally predicts that **experience-based deradicalization (new relationships, new life circumstances, changed environment = changed f) should significantly outperform argument-based deradicalization (counter-narratives = more C processed through unchanged f)**. This is consistent with empirical findings but is *predicted by the framework*, not just observed.

**Novel prediction 2:** r(B) should be trackable during radicalization trajectories. Early stages should show r(B)>0 (genuine recognition of real issues). As ideological A increases, r(B) drops. There should be an identifiable **tipping point** where r(B) crosses into near-zero and the system locks into Class (ii). This predicts a discontinuity in the radicalization process — a phase transition rather than gradual drift.

This is more specific than Moghaddam's staircase (which is purely descriptive) and adds formal dynamics to the 3N model.

**Novel prediction 3:** Theorem 5 (cascade) predicts R_n ≤ R_{n-1} within organizational hierarchies. This formally predicts that **rank-and-file members should have strictly lower R than leadership** — they should be more ideologically rigid, more prone to distortion, less capable of strategic flexibility. This is a known phenomenon but formally derived here as a theorem rather than an observation.

**Existing coverage:** The 3N model captures needs-narratives-networks but doesn't predict which interventions should work. Kruglanski's significance quest captures motivation but not the informational dynamics of entrenchment.

**Assessment:** (c) The Class (ii) stability prediction and the deradicalization modality prediction are falsifiable and novel.

**Depth: DIAGNOSTIC to GENERATIVE** — the formal prediction that architectural change outperforms counter-narratives is genuinely actionable and derived from theory rather than observation.

## Domain 9: IMMUNE SYSTEM

**Existing frameworks:** Burnet's self/non-self theory: the immune system distinguishes self from foreign. Matzinger's danger model: response triggered by damage signals, not foreignness per se. Pradeu's discontinuity theory: response triggered by significant changes in molecular patterns.

**R=C-A application:** The immune system is a biological recognition system. C = molecular pattern contact (antigen encounter, environmental molecular signals). A = processing biases (self-tolerance, regulatory T-cell suppression, prior immune memory as potential distortion). R = accurate immune response (appropriate reaction to genuine threats, tolerance of self).

**Novel prediction: Autoimmunity as mathematical necessity.** Axiom 2 (self-observation floor) applied to the immune system: the immune system self-monitors through regulatory T-cells and tolerance mechanisms. This self-modeling channel necessarily has A* > 0. Therefore **some degree of autoimmune tendency is not a pathological error but a mathematical consequence of any system that models itself to distinguish self from non-self**.

This is a genuinely novel claim:
- Burnet treats autoimmunity as failure of self-recognition
- Matzinger treats it as inappropriate danger signaling
- Pradeu treats it as over-sensitivity to discontinuity

R=C-A predicts autoimmunity is **inevitable** — you cannot build an immune system with self-modeling capacity and zero autoimmune tendency. The prediction is that every functioning immune system exhibits non-zero autoimmune activity, and that this is a design feature (the floor), not a design flaw.

Falsifiable: If an immune system could be engineered with zero autoimmune tendency while maintaining full self-modeling and adaptive recognition capability, this would falsify the prediction.

**Additional prediction:** Theorem 2 classifies immune systems: Class (i) would be perfect immune function (impossible by Axiom 2), Class (ii) is stable partial recognition (most immune systems — some errors are stable), Class (iii) is null (immunodeficiency). The framework predicts a **fundamental tradeoff frontier** between autoimmunity (A too low, over-recognition) and immunodeficiency (A too high, under-recognition), and that this tradeoff cannot be eliminated, only optimized.

**Existing coverage:** The hygiene hypothesis and immune regulation literature acknowledge that the immune system balances tolerance and reactivity, but no existing framework formally proves that zero autoimmunity is impossible for a self-modeling immune system. This is derived from Axiom 2, not from empirical observation.

**Assessment:** (c) Falsifiable and novel — directly generates a testable prediction that no existing immunological framework makes from first principles.

**Depth: GENERATIVE** — the inevitability-of-autoimmunity prediction is a genuine theoretical novelty derived from the framework's axioms.

## Domain 10: DREAMING

**Existing frameworks:** Activation-synthesis (Hobson) treats dreams as post-hoc narrative imposed on random neural firing. Threat simulation (Revonsuo) treats dreams as evolutionary rehearsal. Memory consolidation treats them as side-effects of memory processing. DMN accounts link dreaming to default mode network activity.

**R=C-A application:** During dreaming, external C ≈ 0 (sensory gating). Therefore R = C - A ≈ −A. Dreams are **pure agenda processing** with minimal corrective contact.

**Novel predictions:**
1. Dream content should systematically reflect the dreamer's full A decomposition — not just threats (Revonsuo) or wishes (Freud) but the complete agenda structure: A_bias (motivated distortions, desires, fears) AND A_capacity (processing limitations, representational constraints). This is broader than threat simulation and more precise than "wish fulfillment."

2. Lucid dreaming introduces metacognitive C (awareness of the dream state). R=C-A predicts this small C increase should measurably increase R — lucid dreams should be more *accurate* (in the sense of better reflecting actual concerns rather than distorted projections) than non-lucid dreams. This is testable.

3. If dreams = A, then dream analysis gives a direct readout of one's processing distortions. But Axiom 2 means analyzing your own dreams always has A* > 0 — self-interpretation of dreams necessarily introduces additional distortion. This predicts that **external dream interpretation should be more accurate than self-interpretation**, a testable claim.

**Existing coverage:** The R ≈ −A formulation is clean but Hobson's activation-synthesis already treats dreams as endogenous processing without external correction. The predictive processing account of dreaming (dreams as testing priors without data) is very close to "A without C." The lucid dreaming prediction is interesting but narrow.

**Assessment:** (b)-(c) The lucid dreaming accuracy prediction is testable. The broader formulation is elegant but largely captured by existing frameworks.

**Depth: DIAGNOSTIC** — clean unifying formulation (R≈−A) and the lucid dreaming prediction, but core dynamics are anticipated by activation-synthesis and predictive processing accounts.

## Domain 11: PLACEBO EFFECT

**Existing frameworks:** Expectancy theory attributes placebo to belief in treatment. Classical conditioning attributes it to prior treatment associations. Bayesian brain accounts frame it as prior-driven top-down modulation. Embodied predictive processing treats it as the body implementing expectations.

**R=C-A application:** In placebo, A (expectation/belief) distorts processing — but the distortion produces *genuine physiological benefit*. R = C - A: the person has less accurate apprehension of what's pharmacologically happening (R decreases), but their body responds as if the treatment were real.

**This creates a genuine tension in the framework.** R=C-A implicitly treats A as loss — recognition is reduced by agenda. But placebo shows that A can be *functionally beneficial*. The framework must either:
(a) Accept that low R can sometimes be adaptive (which weakens R's normative status), or
(b) Distinguish between epistemic R and functional outcomes (which adds complexity)

**Novel prediction despite the tension:** R=C-A predicts that placebo effects come at a **recognition cost**. The person gains pain relief but has *less accurate understanding* of causation. This predicts that high placebo responders should show **greater A in general** — more expectation-driven, more agenda-loaded processing across domains, not just medical contexts. There is some evidence linking placebo responsiveness to suggestibility, but R=C-A predicts a broader pattern.

**Theorem 3 prediction:** If A > 0 has a thermodynamic cost (E_A ≥ A · k_B · T · ln 2), then maintaining placebo expectations should have a measurable metabolic cost. This is a genuinely novel and falsifiable prediction — measure metabolic activity during sustained placebo response.

**Existing coverage:** The Bayesian brain account handles placebo more naturally than R=C-A (priors modulating perception is its home territory). The predictive processing account of placebo is more parsimonious. R=C-A is somewhat strained here.

**Assessment:** (b)-(c) The thermodynamic cost prediction is novel but difficult to test. The general-A prediction for placebo responders is testable. But the framework shows visible strain.

**Depth: DIAGNOSTIC** — generates a few novel predictions, but the framework is less natural here than Bayesian/predictive processing accounts. The tension between A-as-loss and A-as-useful is a genuine limitation.

**R=C-A application:** Creative insight = sudden R increase caused by A dropping (a processing constraint is released). Preparation = building C. Incubation = A decreases as conscious agenda relaxes. Illumination = R jumps as the C-A gap opens. Verification = testing the new R against further C.

**Novel prediction 1: Creative block as Class (ii) stable state.** The creator has high C (extensive knowledge, extensive contact with the problem) and high A (agenda about what the solution should look like, career pressures, aesthetic commitments). Theorem 2: this is STABLE. The block is not laziness or lack of input — it's a formal attractor. Escape requires **architectural change to f** — changing medium, changing collaborators, changing environment, changing the constraints themselves. This predicts that changing processing architecture should break creative blocks more reliably than intensified effort within the same architecture.

**Novel prediction 2:** Theorem 5 predicts declining originality within artistic/intellectual lineages: R_n ≤ R_{n-1} for chains of influence. Artistic movements should become progressively more mannered and academic until fresh C from an independent source disrupts the cascade. This is observed (mannerism following every major style) but formally derived here.

**Novel prediction 3:** Theorem 6 (Meta-Recursive Generativity) predicts that the deepest creative insights are those where the framework itself is modified — meta-creative acts that change the space of possible creations, not just produce new instances within the existing space. This predicts a formal hierarchy of creative depth that maps to observed distinctions (craft vs. art vs. paradigm-shifting art).

**Existing coverage:** The free energy account already treats insight as sudden prediction-error resolution, which is close to "A drops, R jumps." Wallas's stages are well-established. The lineage-decline prediction is informally observed.

**Assessment:** (b)-(c) The Class (ii) block prediction is testable. The lineage prediction is already informally known. The meta-creative hierarchy is interesting but difficult to operationalize.

**Depth: DIAGNOSTIC** — the Class (ii) stability of creative block is a useful and non-trivial formalization. The cascade and meta-generativity predictions add precision. But the core insight mechanism is already well-described by predictive processing / free energy accounts.

## 2. Pattern: Where R=C-A Adds Depth vs. Redescribes

R=C-A cuts deepest when two conditions are met:

**Condition A: Self-modeling is central to the phenomenon.** Axiom 2 (self-observation floor) is the framework's most powerful weapon. It generates formal impossibility results — hard ceilings that existing frameworks don't prove. This is why it scored GENERATIVE on psychotherapy (self-knowledge has a floor), immune system (self-tolerance has a floor), and falling in love (mutual recognition has a floor). Every domain where the phenomenon essentially involves a system modeling itself is a domain where Axiom 2 generates novel predictions.

**Condition B: Stuck states are the puzzle.** Theorem 2 (Class (ii) stability) explains why certain conditions are stable attractors rather than failures of effort. This is why it scored well on addiction, radicalization, creative block — all domains where the central puzzle is "why can't you just think your way out?"

**Where it adds least:** Domains already well-served by information-theoretic or Bayesian frameworks (placebo, market bubbles) get less novelty because R=C-A's formalism covers ground those frameworks already formalize. The placebo domain actually *strains* the framework by showing A can be functional.

## 3. The Framework's Unique Contribution

Three things no single existing framework provides in combination:

1. **Impossibility proofs from self-observation (Axiom 2).** This is the crown jewel. The formal proof that any self-modeling system has A* > 0 generates hard ceilings across domains — therapeutic progress, immune tolerance, mutual knowledge in relationships, meditative attainment, propaganda resistance. No existing domain-specific framework proves these impossibility results from first principles.

2. **Stability classification of recognition states (Theorem 2).** The three-class taxonomy (Full / Partial / Null) with the key insight that **Class (ii) is stable** provides a formal account of stuck states that transcends domain-specific explanations. Addiction, radicalization, creative block, paradigmatic science — all Class (ii) stability.

3. **The diagnostic metric r(B).** The marginal recognition return on contact provides a single quantity that is meaningful across all domains — when r→0, you know the bottleneck is A, not C. This is potentially operationalizable and provides cross-domain comparability.

## 4. Verdict: Unifying Framework or Useful Lens?

**R=C-A earns a qualified claim to unifying framework status — but with an important caveat.**

*In its favor:* It is not merely a lens that redescribes. Zero domains scored SURFACE. Three scored GENERATIVE — producing predictions that no existing domain-specific framework makes. Nine scored DIAGNOSTIC — identifying something specific that existing frameworks miss or don't formalize. That is strong performance across twelve diverse domains from neuroscience to immunology to economics.

The unifying power comes from a small axiom set generating cross-domain predictions. Axiom 2 alone generates novel results in at least five domains. This is the mark of a genuine theoretical contribution, not just a metaphor.

*The caveat:* The framework's normative commitment — A is always loss, R is always the goal — creates genuine strain in domains where "distortion" is functional (placebo, falling in love's initial bonding phase, useful simplification in creative work). A fully mature version would need to distinguish between **epistemic R** (accuracy of apprehension) and **functional adequacy** (adaptive utility of the processing outcome). The current framework treats R as univocally good, but several domains reveal that sometimes A serves the organism even as it reduces R.

*The honest bottom line:* R=C-A is more than a useful lens but less than a finished unifying theory. It is a **generative meta-framework** — a small formal structure that, when applied to specific domains, reliably produces insights and predictions that domain-specific frameworks miss. Its power center is Axiom 2, and its diagnostic reach comes from the Theorem 2 stability classification. Where it encounters self-modeling systems with stable processing architectures, it cuts deeper than anything else available. Where it encounters functional distortion or domains already well-served by Bayesian formalism, it merely adds a complementary perspective.

Three generative, nine diagnostic, zero surface — across twelve domains with no cherry-picking — is a strong result. The framework earns its keep.
